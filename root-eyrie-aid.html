03PRIORITY 1Decree — The 5 Maintenance Rules

Every card added is a dependency. The Decree's safety differs sharply by column. Treat columns asymmetrically.

BGG Decree Rules

1. Recruit is a trap
Two failure modes: run out of warriors or get turmoiled because opponents kill your only roost in that suit (you can't prevent it on your turn).
Never put Recruit into a suit where you have only 1 roost.
2. Move is nearly always safe
Eyrie almost never turmoils on Move. When in doubt, dump cards there.
3. Push 2 cards/turn into the Decree
More actions = more momentum. Even just 2× Move is fine. Ambition pays.
4. Don't lean on Bird cards
The fewer Birds in the Decree, the cheaper Turmoil is. Hoard them for emergencies (suit-locks, crafting saves, post-Turmoil restarts).
5. Turmoil is OK if planned
Turmoil on Build with few Birds = small loss. With all roosts down you snap most VP back instantly.

Targeting & Supply Checks

Battle-suit
Add only with a guaranteed target: stationary Sawmill, Sympathy, defenseless Vagabond, or a clearing you reliably reach.
Build-suit
Sequence a Move before it if no current empty slot.
If  UnitsSupply < UnitsDecree-Recruit  → CRASH

02PRIORITY 2Leader Sequencing

Leaders aren't picked in isolation — they form a sequence. Default arc: Charismatic (open) → Commander or Despot (close).

Charismatic 1st choice
The Engine — Open With This
+1 recruit per Recruit action. Get all your roosts & warriors out fast, then turmoil early to swap leader.
2-card opening: 1 card matching home suit (→ Move) + 1 card matching the suit shared by 2 adjacent clearings (→ Build). Won't turmoil until end of T4.
Falls off: late-game, too many warriors → recruit-turmoil risk.
Builder Backup open
The Stabilizer
Bird-in-recruit and bird-in-move are safe defaults. Score VP via crafted items.
Use when: cards don't enable Charismatic (no home-suit / no shared-suit pair). Slow down, recruit more, lean on crafts.
Don't: use Builder late unless sitting on unbuilt high-VP crafts.
Commander 1st follow-up
The Enforcer — Classic Charismatic Follow-up
+1 Hit in attack. Eyrie is the only faction that can land 5 hits in one combat.
Use when: roosts already down → you've won, just protect the board. Or open with it to dent a stubborn faction. Synergy: Brutal Tactics + Commander = 2 free hits, applied after rolls.
Despot Late swap
The Scaler — VP From Cardboard
+1 VP per building/token you remove in battle.
Open with Despot only for: a no-turmoil game vs token-heavy factions (Alliance — sympathy = "VP biscuits"). Don't open with Despot just for the bird-in-build then swap — not enough warriors out.
Best swap target: after Charismatic if you didn't get all roosts down.

09PRIORITY 3Pre-Turn Audit

Run at start of every Birdsong. Any "no" = fix before adding to Decree.

1 Recruit safety
≥2 roosts in that suit? Buffer warriors in supply (2–3)?
2 Targeting
Battle/Build has a real target? Or sequence Move first.
3 Bird discipline
Holding ≥1 Bird for emergencies? Don't dump them all into Decree.
4 External Threat
Can WA Revolt, Vagabond Strike, or Otter hit-squad pop a critical roost?
5 Turmoil exit
If I turmoil now, do I have a Bird to relaunch and a roost to score from?

05PRIORITY 4Combat — You Hit Hardest

5-hit ceiling Eyrie is the only faction that can land 5 hits in one combat (Commander +1 + Brutal Tactics +1 + dice). Use it on stubborn defenders.
Rule of 1 Control
Single warrior rules on ties (Lords of the Forest). Spread thin to maximize action coverage.
Rule of 3 Durability
Vs Marquise / Duchy / Otter hit-squads: upgrade key roosts to 2–3 warriors.

Killer Synergies

  • Brutal Tactics + Commander = 2 free hits, applied after the rolls. Insane vs Alliance.
  • Armorers / Sappers = defend roosts without burning Recruit actions.

Mandatory Battle Rule

Never Take a mandatory battle unless success is near-certain. A failed mandatory = forced Turmoil.
Vs Lizards Always kill the Garden, not just warriors. Killing only their warriors helps them.

★PRIORITY 5Common Crash Patterns

Avoid these = Turmoil spirals stop.

1 Recruit on a 1-roost suit
One opponent strike → guaranteed Turmoil.
2 Bird-heavy Decree
Turmoil cost balloons; you also lose your emergency currency.
3 Crafting Birds for 1 VP
Save them for suit-locks & post-Turmoil restarts.
4 Despot-first & swap
Not enough warriors out — pick Charismatic or Builder to open.
5 Turmoil with empty hand
No Bird = dead rounds. Hold one before pulling the trigger.

01Core Philosophy

Reframe
Eyrie is NOT a maximization problem ("how many actions?"). It is a Constraint Satisfaction Problem.

Target

  • Maintain 4–5 actions (stable, minimally sufficient)
  • Convert board presence → consistent VP

Overclock (6+ actions)

Only in the Sprint Phase — last 2 turns, when victory is mathematically guaranteed.
CORE

04Triangle Strategy

Abandon linear expansion. Aim for Triangle Control — rule 3 adjacent clearings of different suits (Fox / Rabbit / Mouse).

Redundancy If one node is blocked, the other two provide valid Move/Battle targets.
Radial Mobility Reach more of the map with fewer Move actions.
Stability Resists suit-locking by opponents.
CORE

06Card Economy

Principle Hand = Error Correction Bandwidth.

Bird Valuation

Treat Birds as gold. Never craft them for 1 VP.
Save to: fix a suit-locked Decree · recover post-Turmoil.

Hand Filtering

  • Hold: high-value crafts (Builder) · Birds
  • Dump: suits that don't match your Triangle

Throughput

Build Roosts early → more cards/turn → more options to pivot Decree.
CORE

07Controlled Turmoil

Turmoil is a strategic RESET, not a failure.

Cheap Turmoils

  • Build column, few Birds — minimal VP loss; roosts already on board snap most VP back
  • Charismatic → Commander or Despot (after roosts down)

Expensive Turmoils — avoid

  • Recruit-suit Turmoil — usually means you ran out of warriors or lost your roosts. Often unrecoverable.
  • Bird-heavy Decree — every Bird discarded = a planned action you'll never do.

Requirements before pulling the trigger

  • ≥1 Bird in hand to relaunch
  • Roosts already mostly down (so VP recovery is fast)
  • Next leader chosen, fits the board state

★Crafting — Upgrades Beat Items

Item crafting is opportunistic — most plays are 1-VP filler. Upgrade crafting is where Eyrie gets real leverage. Don't conflate the two.

Brutal Tactics
S-Tier Synergy
+1 Hit per battle, opponent gets +1 VP. Stack with Commander for 2 free hits, applied after the rolls.
Devastating vs Alliance bases (≤2 warriors).
Tax Collectors
Decree Pressure-Valve
Trim 1 excess warrior/turn → delays Recruit-Turmoil. Bonus card draw.
Use when: Charismatic late game, supply tightening.
Armorers / Sappers
Defensive Plates
Negate enemy hits → defend roosts without burning Recruit. Wasteful for Cats — fine for you.
Use when: facing repeated Marquise / Duchy assaults.
Royal Claim / Stand & Deliver
Bird-Card Misc
Situational; only craft if a Bird is otherwise dead weight in hand.
Don't craft Birds for 1 VP items — your error currency.
Vagabond watch Don't feed their crafting kit. If you're ahead on items, slow-roll the next item-craft.
CORE

★Matchups

Marquise (Cats)
Frenemy. Mid-game, you both benefit from suppressing minor factions — quietly cooperate. End-game, your roosts make you a target. Brutal Tactics + Commander can shred their recruiters or even the Keep.
Woodland Alliance
Sympathy = "VP biscuits" for Despot. Eat 1–2/turn, but don't get greedy — Alliance scoring on Outrage/Mobilize bursts hard if they're flush with cards.
Hit bases of ≤2 warriors with Commander/Brutal Tactics. ≥3 warriors = they've already won the fight.
Lizard Cult
Always kill the Garden, not just warriors. Killing only warriors helps them recycle the outcast suit. Plan attacks so the building dies in the same battle.
Riverfolk (Otter)
Otter hit-squads ferry warriors deep to pop undefended roosts in one action. Always 2+ warriors on isolated roosts that border rivers.
Vagabond
Same threat model as Otter — strikes undefended roosts. Hostile = your territory becomes molasses for them. Don't craft items that fuel their kit.
Corvid / Duchy / Keepers
Generic playbook: protect roosts with Rule of 3, watch for plot bombs, race their unique scoring path. Brutal Tactics + Commander handles tunnels & waystations.

08Map-Specific Adaptations

The map dictates the "shape" of your script.

Autumn LOW
Ideal for Triangle Strategy. Maximum flexibility.
Winter MEDIUM
Higher variance — suits are randomized per card. Prioritize Birds.
Lake MEDIUM
Use the Ferry for mobility redundancy and surprise Triangle shifts.
Mountain EXTREME
Anti-Eyrie map. Choke points + forced linear pathing make TRT much harder.
Mountain Survival
  • Do NOT attempt a large Triangle
  • Focus on holding the Pass
  • Prioritize Commander to force through blocked nodes

★Advanced Tactics

Hand-first leader pick
Before flipping a leader, simulate turn 1's Decree: do you have movement out of your start? If not, don't pick Charismatic — you'll turmoil immediately.
Battle = warrior-shedder
Use Battle actions to burn excess warriors and prevent Recruit-Turmoil from a swelling supply (similar to Tax Collectors but on demand).
Move = uncertainty buffer
When unsure of next-turn legality, route the card into Move. It almost never causes Turmoil and rarely costs tempo.
Rule, don't eliminate
You only need to rule a clearing to place a roost — eliminating all enemy warriors isn't required. Cheaper than it looks.
Decree as table-talk shield
"Nothing personal — fox clearing was on my Decree." Use mandatory framing to deflect heat from aggressive plays.
Royal Claim = discard scoring
Royal Claim scores on discard, not on play. Pairs cleanly with any leader's workflow.
Tournament data
Tracked-game data: Charismatic 71% / Despot 29% opening picks. Turmoil correlated with 0 wins in one dataset — even a "controlled" Turmoil is high-risk. Plan it, don't romanticize it.
Don't telegraph weak roosts
Lone roosts on river/forest edges advertise to Otters/Vagabond. Either fortify (Rule of 3) or accept the loss in advance.

★Probability & Variance

Risk Assessment

Never take a mandatory battle unless success is near-certain. A failed mandatory battle forces Turmoil.

Variance Reduction

  • Dice rolls 0-0 and 1-0 cause failure cascades
  • Commander's +1 Hit eliminates the 0-hit outcome entirely
  • Despot + Commander pairing = best variance profile for mid-game sweep
P(kill | Commander) ≫ P(kill | Builder)
