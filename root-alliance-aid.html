01PRIORITY 1Be Invisible

If anyone says "stop the Alliance," you've already lost.

Alliance scores by efficiently spending cards — a path that's obvious once visible. Your job is to look harmless until the win is locked in. The right Alliance game ends with one player suddenly going from 15 → 30 VP.

Three threats to manage

Threat Looking like the leader → coalition forms.
Threat Visible card hoard → "she's holding 8 cards, kill her base."
Threat Provoking the Cats early → choke-out blockades.

How to disappear

  • Stay just behind in score; let Eyrie/Cats trade leadership
  • Show only 1–2 bases, modest officers — not your card stash
  • Play up "I only have 4 cards, I'm fine" table-talk
  • Frame your sympathy placement as "someone else's problem" — sympathy on Eyrie's path? "I'm just hassling them, not you"
ESSENTIAL

02PRIORITY 2First Base — Land It Safely

Goal of turns 1–2: 1 base on the board, alive. Everything else flows from this.

Pre-Turn-1 evaluation (6 cards: 3 hand + 3 supporters)

  1. Group all 6 cards by suit
  2. Identify which suits have 2+ revolt opportunities
  3. Pick a target suit that gives you at least 2 backup revolts if turn 2 is contested

Clearing Selection

Look for
  • Central, well-connected (3+ paths)
  • Few enemy buildings/warriors
  • Far from Cat Keep / Eyrie home
Avoid
  • 2-path corner clearings (bottleneck)
  • Adjacent to enemy stronghold
  • Clearings Vagabond/Riverfolk view as free VP

Turn 1 Default Pattern

Birdsong Place sympathy in target clearing
Daylight Craft if possible (mouse/rabbit only)
Evening Move officers, dump remaining cards into supporters
Hold revolt for T2 unless you have a favor card, Ambush, or S-tier craft (Armorers, BBB) requiring it sooner.

04PRIORITY 4The Big Turn

15 → 30 VP in one turn is the Alliance win shape.

You don't grind to 30. You hover at ~15, enter the endgame around ~20 VP, then burst into the 3-cost sympathy track where most of your VP lives.

The Sequence

Birdsong Spread 2–3 sympathy from your supporters
Daylight Craft off the new sympathy clearings (favor / VP items)
Evening Move base defenders out, organize them into more sympathy

Setup requirements

  • 1–2 bases secure (Armorers ideally crafted)
  • 5+ supporter cards stockpiled, mixed suits
  • Clearings of 2–3 different suits within reach
  • Officers staged for organize chains (2 minimum for Move+Organize chain)
Don't telegraph Don't let your supporter pile cross 8–9 cards visibly. People notice.
Score milestones 0–10: invisible · 10–20: building · 20+: load the cannon · 25+: fire it.

06Crafting Priorities

Alliance crafting is suit-flexible — you don't need workshops, just sympathy. Lean mouse / rabbit for VP cards. Avoid fox VP cards (best Vagabond items).

S-Tier Upgrades

Armorers Negate enemy hits. With Guerrilla War: invincible base defense.
Better Burrow Bank More cards = more VP for Alliance. You convert cards to points faster than anyone.
Tax Collectors 1 warrior → 1 card. Yes you're low on warriors — but you'll just recruit one back, and the card is worth it.
Sappers Stack with Armorers + Guerrilla War. Bases become untouchable.

Anti-Ambush Tools

Scouting Party Negates Ambush — important since you'll occasionally have to attack
Codebreakers See opponents' hands; spot Ambush threats before committing

Sympathy-Before-Craft Trick

Order Birdsong (place sympathy) → Daylight (craft using that clearing's suit) lets you set up a favor card requirement and use it in one turn. Plan multi-turn favor setups.
CORE

07Sympathy Math & Placement

Each card spent on sympathy ≈ 1 VP. If an opponent destroys it, they paid in actions; you paid almost nothing.

Maximize VP/card

  • Spread to clearings matching your supporter suits — you organize with the suit you have, not what's on the board
  • Bird cards: save for matching same-suit-as-base spreads (vs Lizards) or rescues
  • Look for "free card" opportunities — sympathy on a clearing the Eyrie must battle through (Outrage trigger)

Provocation patterns

Sympathy as bait Place on clearings opponents are forced through. Outrage gives you a card every time they remove or move-attack it. Sympathy that survives 1 attack already paid 2x.
Wood-stockpile signal A Cat clearing with stockpiled wood is a juicy revolt target — destroying that wood is a heavy points swing for you and a major loss for them.
CORE

08Officers & Bases

Officer Targets

  • 2 officers minimum ASAP — unlocks Move + Organize chain in Evening, accelerating sympathy spread and 2nd-base deployment
  • ~5 officers by mid-game — enough actions to chain spread/organize
  • Beyond 5: diminishing returns; each officer = 1 less defender

Base Defense Floor

2 warriors + Armorers Most opponents won't bother. The ROI for them is awful.
1 warrior + Armorers Tempting target. Acceptable for short windows.
0 warriors Free destruction. Don't.

Reserve a Base for Adjacency

Trick Hold off building your second base until you can place it adjacent to a future revolt clearing. Now organization can flow outward through that adjacency.
CORE

09Time-Bomb Revolt

After base 1 lands, switch from safe sympathy to aggressive sympathy in important enemy clearings.

The Pattern (4 officers minimum)

  1. Birdsong: spread sympathy into a key enemy clearing
  2. Daylight: move 2 warriors there (officer chain), organize for a base if possible
  3. Evening: recruit a warrior back at base to keep it safe
Result A defended sympathy/base in their face. They can pour warriors at it — Guerrilla War means their warriors die more than yours. Add Ambush + Armorers and it's a dagger they can't pull out.

Where to plant

  • Eyrie's forced battle/move/build clearing (Outrage card stream)
  • Cat wood stockpile clearing (huge VP swing)
  • Lizard's outcast-mismatch clearing (denies sanctify)

11Matchups

Marquise (Cats)
Threat: Choke-out — they can dump 5+ warriors in your base clearing as a blockade.
Counter: Don't be their biggest threat. Reserve a 2nd base adjacent to revolt clearings for outflow paths. Look for wood stockpiles — revolting on those is a massive VP swing for you, massive loss for them.
Eyrie
Both threat and farm. Their static Decree forces them through specific clearings — plant sympathy there for an Outrage card-stream.
Migrate away as they grow strong. Commander negates Guerrilla War; Brutal Tactics + Commander = your ability nullified. Exit their orbit before that combo lands. Despot eats your sympathy for VP — they get points but feed you cards (calculate the trade).
Vagabond
Threat: Crossbow evens GW odds. Scoundrel's Scorched Earth = nuclear (once/game). Multi-attack turns.
Counter: Befriend them — craft weak items as Aid (mutually beneficial VP). Never craft fox VP cards (their best gear). Let bigger factions handle them. If they go Hostile you become their VP buffet.
Riverfolk
Minimal interaction. NEVER buy services — they keep your warriors permanently. You're terrible revolt targets for each other.
Action: Make sure someone at the table polices them. Talk down opponents who want to over-buy from Riverfolk.
Corvid Conspiracy
Plot bombs near your bases = catastrophic. Codebreakers helps you scout. Expose plots if you can spare actions; don't stack a base with everything you've got.
Duchy / Keepers
Aggressive military factions. Treat like Cats: don't be biggest threat, hold defensive layers, exploit their crown/relic obsession to plant sympathy on their forced paths.

12Lizard Counter (Key Matchup)

Lizards vs Alliance is a high-stakes mirror — both can wreck the other. The ball is in your court if you play it right.

Core Rule

Never let them sanctify you. Stay on ONE base. Manage the outcast suit.

Outcast Suit Manipulation

  • You burn more cards than anyone — you control the outcast suit better than they do
  • Spread sympathy to clearings of different suit from your base
  • Use bird cards to spread to clearings matching your base suit
  • Train with bird cards when possible
  • Craft liberally — denies them items and shifts outcast cycling

Why You Hurt Them So Badly

  • Revolt kills don't become acolytes. You vaporize their warriors AND their gardens with no compensation
  • Each sympathy of yours costs them 1–2 acolytes to even attack — and they still lose warriors in combat
  • Forcing them to move warriors from adjacent clearings = several cards spent to do something you do for nothing
  • They run out of acolytes faster than you run out of sympathy
Risk Other players can collude on the outcast suit. But removing your single base while you keep cycling cards is a tall order.

14Advanced Tactics

Outrage card-pump
Plant sympathy in a clearing the Eyrie's Decree forces them to battle/move through. They feed you a card every time they trigger Outrage. Free engine.
Ambush insurance
Hold an Ambush card in hand vs aggressive factions. The threat alone deters attacks; revealing it once usually pays for itself.
Adjacency for organize
Plan base #2 placement so it's adjacent to your next revolt target. You can organize outward through it — multiplies your action range.
Vagabond aid as VP trade
Slip the Vagabond weak items via Aid for mutual VP. Avoids Hostile state where they harvest your sympathy.
Bird card discipline
Bird cards are flexible suit-fillers. Save for: same-suit spreads (vs Lizards), Outrage retaliation, big-turn organize chains. Don't waste them.
Card hoard ceiling
~6 cards in supporters = invisible. ~9+ = "she's about to pop" alarm bells. Spread some sympathy as a release valve before crossing the threshold.
Free-turn fishing
Sometimes the best Birdsong is just placing 2 sympathy and ending. If no one's punishing you, don't burn cards for marginal VP.
Don't revolt the Riverfolk
They have no buildings worth your effort. Spend that revolt on a Cat/Eyrie clearing instead.
Phase transitions
Phase 1 (T1–3): land base 1, accumulate cards. Phase 2 (T4–6): expand sympathy wide, add 2nd base. Phase 3 (T7+): expect coordinated attacks — plan damage mitigation, not full prevention. Phase 4 (final 1–2 turns): big-turn burst.
Stand and Deliver
Card-draw upgrade — steal a card from a player with more cards than you. Pairs perfectly with your "always have fewer cards visibly" posture.
Mild inconvenience > severe pain
Sympathy that mildly bothers everyone beats sympathy that maims one player. Even threat distribution = no one prioritizes you.
3 sympathy on T1 if possible
High-tempo opening: 3 tokens beats 2 if board allows. Forces immediate threat spread across multiple opponents.
