01PRIORITY 1Be the Wanderer

You're not a faction — you're one character carrying gear, alone in the woods.

What makes you different: Every other faction has buildings, warriors, an army. You have one piece on the board (your meeple) and a personal inventory of items. The items are your faction. Lose your items and you're nothing. Build your items and you're potentially the strongest player at the table.

TL;DR. Spend turns 1–4 hitting the 4 ruins to grab their items. Once you have 2+ swords, transition to scoring via combat (Infamy), Aid, and Quests. You can quietly snowball into a win — but only if no one notices.

Why people don't see you coming

"No one is looking at player boards to see what is going on there, and with the Vagabond your player board is everything you have." Most players track factions by board presence — you have almost none. So when you start unloading items in a single turn, opponents are caught off guard.

Three threats to manage

Threat 1 Early aggression. Before you have swords, any faction that ends a turn with units in your clearing can punch you for free. Damaged items mean you lose a turn licking wounds.
Threat 2 Becoming visible. The moment players realize how much VP you're sitting on, they coalition. Don't telegraph item count.
Threat 3 The Riverfolk stack. Otters can drop 4 warriors in one clearing in a single action. If you ended your turn there, you're toast.

Three ways you score

  • Crafting items (1 VP per item) — modest but constant
  • Aid → Friendship (give cards to factions, eventually score VP per aid) — steady
  • Combat (Infamy) — once Hostile to a faction, every warrior you kill = 1 VP. With 3 swords this snowballs hard
  • Quests — opportunistic 2 VP each (or a card)
The hidden truth: Vagabond wins look easy because by the time anyone notices, you've already won. But Vagabond losses happen when one faction realizes turn 3 that you're vulnerable and beats you up before you collect any swords. The early game is a stealth phase, not a combat phase.

02PRIORITY 2Anatomy of a Turn

Vagabond turns work very differently from other factions. There's no army to manage, no buildings to track. Instead, your turn is about spending items as currency to take actions — each action exhausts the matching item, which then has to be refreshed.

Birdsong
Start of turn
  • Refresh items (default = 3, more with Tea)
  • Slip: 1 free movement to an adjacent clearing OR a forest (doesn't trigger Hostile — unique to Vagabond)
  • (Activate Royal Claim if crafted, etc.)
Daylight
Middle of turn
  • Take any number of actions, each exhausts an item:
  • · Move (boots) · Battle (sword) · Strike (crossbow)
  • · Aid (give card to faction) · Quest (spend matching items)
  • · Craft (use hammers + cards) · Repair (torch)
  • · Special action (depends on Vagabond class)
Evening
End of turn
  • Lick wounds: repair 1 damaged item if you're alone in a forest
  • Draw cards (1 + bonus per friendship + Draw Bonus)
  • Discard hand to 5 if over

How items work as currency

Each item starts refreshed (face-up). When you take an action, you flip a matching item to exhausted (face-down). At start of next turn's Birdsong, you refresh up to 3 items (more if you have Tea). So having more total items lets you take more actions per turn — but only if you can refresh them all.

The damaged track — your lifeline

When opponents attack you in battle, hits don't kill warriors (you have none); they damage your items. Damaged items move to a separate "damaged" track and can't be used or refreshed. To get them back, you need to:

  • Use a Repair action (costs 1 hammer + 1 matching-color card to fix 1 item)
  • Or use the Ranger's Hideout ability (repair 3 items at once)
  • Or lick wounds in Evening (repair 1 item if alone in a forest — slow but free)
Why this matters: Damaged items mean lost actions per turn. If you're at 2 swords + 1 boot + 2 hammers and someone deals 3 damage, you might lose your sword AND your boot, leaving you stranded. Item count = action budget = win condition.
Picture this — turn 1
You start as the Thief with a torch, boots, and a sword. Your starting clearing is a fox clearing with a ruin.

Birdsong: Refresh items (already refreshed turn 1).
Daylight: Explore the ruin (1 free torch action) — gain a hammer item. Move (boot) to adjacent clearing. Attempt to craft a 1-VP item if you have a matching card.
Evening: Draw 1 card.
End state: 4 items (torch, boot, sword, hammer), in good position to hit ruin #2 next turn.

03PRIORITY 3Pick Your Vagabond

There are 6 Vagabond characters, each with different starting items and a unique special ability. Your choice shapes your entire strategy. Tier ratings reflect the BGG strategy guide's assessment.

Arbiter S-tier
The Consistent Powerhouse
Start: torch, boots, 2 swords
Ability — Protector: can defend another player in their battle (rarely used)
Why pick: 2 swords means you skip the entire "vulnerable opening" phase that plagues other Vagabonds. You can score Infamy from the very first turn if you find a target. After looting one ruin sword you have 3 swords, which is the soft "win condition" threshold. Most consistently strong Vagabond per BGG.
Ranger A-tier
The Combat Build
Start: torch, boots, sword, crossbow
Ability — Hideout: repair 3 items at once
Why pick: Sword + crossbow gives you both attack power and ranged poke (crossbow has no return fire). Hideout means you don't fear damage as much — repair 3 items in one action. Pick a target faction early and grind Infamy.
Thief (Roger) A-tier
The Balanced Default
Start: torch, boots, sword, tea
Ability — Steal: take a random card from a player in your clearing
Why pick: The canonical Vagabond. Sword for safety, tea means you can refresh 4+ items per turn (no need to hoard). Pair with Codebreakers (see opponent hands) to know what you'll Steal. Balanced and forgiving.
Tinker B-tier (high ceiling)
The Spoilt Crafter
Start: torch, boots, hammer, bag
Ability — Tinker: search the discard pile for a card
Why pick: Starts with the hammer (rare) and can pull any discarded card. Only Vagabond capable of crafting Favor cards. Extra bag means more items.
The catch: No starting sword = high vulnerability. Skilled opponents will rush you. Pick this only if your group won't gang up on the obvious threat.
Scoundrel B-tier (situational)
The Fire-Setter
Start: torch, boots
Ability — Scorched Earth: destroy ALL pieces in your clearing (once per game)
Why pick: Coolest ability in the game. Wipes a Cat clearing with stockpiled wood = massive VP swing. Strong as a coalition partner (cuts off enemy paths).
The catch: No swords = early target. Sadly Scorched Earth scores no Infamy directly (the killed warriors don't count). A specialty pick.
Vagrant C-tier
The Infamy Specialist
Start: torch, boots
Ability — Instigate: force two hostile factions to battle each other
Why pick: The only Vagabond that scores Infamy off Instigate kills (where neither faction loses items). High Infamy ceiling — up to 6 hits if forced battle goes well.
The catch: No swords AND no extra items = doubly vulnerable in opening. Arbiter reaches similar Infamy with way less risk. Speculative pick.
BGG verdict: Arbiter is the safest competitive pick (consistent strong opening). Tinker has highest peak power but only if your group lets you live. Avoid Vagrant unless you specifically want the Instigate gimmick.

04PRIORITY 4Get Stuff (Opening Turns 1–4)

Turns 1–4: visit all 4 ruins, extract items, stay alive.

The opening for every Vagabond character is roughly the same: systematically loot the 4 ruins on the map. Each ruin contains an item. Two of them have a hammer and one has a sword — these are the priority. Once you have a hammer, you can craft items (which gives you VP + more items). Once you have 2 swords, opponents stop seeing you as easy prey.

TL;DR. Visit ruins in T1–4. Prioritize routes that pass through hammer/sword ruins first. Avoid Riverfolk/Eyrie/Cat clusters. End each turn somewhere safe (sympathy clearing, isolated forest).

Ruin priority

  • Sword ruin (#1 priority if no starting sword) — turns you from soft target to viable
  • Hammer ruin — unlocks crafting (1 VP + items per craft = compounding)
  • Other ruins (boots / tea) — useful but not urgent
Why ruins specifically: Each ruin has the Explore action — Vagabond ability that yields the item inside. Ruins are pre-placed at game start (4 of them). Once looted, the ruin is gone. So your goal is "be the first to all 4." Other factions can't loot ruins, so this is uncontested.

Avoiding the early beatdown

Without swords, your defenses are: lots of items (each absorbs 1 hit), licking wounds, and avoidance. The smart play is mostly avoidance.

Don't end your turn
  • In a clearing with 2+ Riverfolk warriors — they'll spend an action to mess you up for nothing
  • Where you think the Eyrie's Decree forces them next turn — they're going to battle there anyway
  • In a Cat recruiter cluster — Cats with 4+ warriors are tempting Vagabond hunters
Safe haven options
  • Clearings with Alliance sympathy and few warriors — opponents avoid these (Outrage triggers)
  • Forests — Slip lets you move to a forest for free, and you can lick wounds there
  • Empty clearings — boring but safe
Example opening route
You start in a fox clearing (no ruin). Two ruins are 1 move away (rabbit + mouse), one is 2 moves (mouse). Hand: 2 fox cards.
T1: Move to the rabbit ruin. Explore → gain hammer. End turn.
T2: Move to mouse ruin. Explore → gain boots. Move again. Craft a 1-VP item using your fox cards if possible.
T3: Move toward the second mouse ruin. Explore → gain sword. Now you have 2 swords (counting starter). Safe.
T4: Last ruin (across the map). Explore → tea or whatever's left. Now fully kitted and ready to score.

05PRIORITY 5Combat Math & Infamy

Combat is your highest-throughput scoring path once you have 3+ swords. But the math is non-obvious — let's break it down.

TL;DR. 1 sword = 1 Battle action per turn. Each Battle scores 1 VP per warrior killed (Infamy) IF you're Hostile to that faction. With 3 swords + Hostile target = 6+ VP per turn. Crossbow is bonus (1 free hit, no counter, but turns you Hostile).

How swords work

  • Each Battle action exhausts 1 sword. So 3 swords = 3 Battles per turn (if you can refresh them all)
  • Your hits in battle = number of swords you have (refreshed or exhausted)
  • So 3 swords means you roll dice + add 0 (Vagabond doesn't get army-size bonus); you take the higher die as attacker

What is Infamy?

Infamy is your VP-from-combat track. Each warrior you kill in battle scores you VP — but only if you're already Hostile to that faction. You become Hostile by attacking that faction without Aiding them recently. Once Hostile:

  • 1 VP per warrior killed (most Vagabonds)
  • Vagrant: 2 VP per warrior killed if BOTH factions are hostile to you (Instigate)

The throughput math

With 3 swords + Hostile target faction in a clearing with 3 warriors:

  • Battle 1: kill ~2 warriors (depends on dice; roughly 2 hits average) = 2 VP
  • Battle 2: kill 1 more (may need to wait, opponent may move) = 1 VP
  • Battle 3: opportunistic = 1+ VP

Realistic per-turn yield: ~6 VP from combat. Plus you're denying that faction warriors. Compare to Aid (~2 VP/turn maxed out) or Crafting (~1 VP per item, not every turn).

Crossbow (Strike action)

Strike The crossbow lets you deal 1 free hit to any clearing you can reach, with no counter-attack. Costs 1 crossbow exhaustion. Doesn't score Infamy directly, but kills the warrior — and if you were already Hostile, that hit's killing the warrior counts.

The crossbow trick: If you want to become Hostile to a faction, kill one warrior with the crossbow. You're now Hostile. From this turn forward, your sword attacks score Infamy.

Why combat scoring beats other paths: Crafting caps at 1 VP per item (and items are limited). Aid caps at ~3 VP per max-friendship per game. Quests cap at 2 VP each. Combat scales with sword count and target density — a Lizard with 6 acolytes/warriors in one cluster = 6+ VP for you to harvest. No other path scales this way.
Picture this — Infamy farming the Lizards
You're Arbiter at turn 7 with 4 swords (2 starting + 2 from ruins/aid). The Lizards have 4 warriors stacked on a lightly-defended garden. You've previously become Hostile to them by attacking once.

Daylight: Move into Lizard clearing (1 boot). Battle: kill 2 warriors → +2 VP. Battle: kill 1 + the garden building → +2 VP. Battle: kill last warrior → +1 VP. Total: 5 VP this turn from combat alone.

The Lizards now have to spend acolytes to recover. You move on. Repeat next turn against another lightly-defended target. This is your win condition.

06PRIORITY 65 Failure Modes (and What to Do Instead)

If you've lost as Vagabond, it was almost certainly one of these. Fix them and your win rate jumps.

1 Getting attacked T2 with no sword
The mistake: Picked Tinker/Scoundrel/Vagrant, ended turn 1 in a clearing with the Riverfolk. They send 3 warriors and damage half your items. Turn 2 = lick wounds = lost game.
Instead: If you have no starting sword, end turn 1 in a forest, an empty clearing, or an Alliance sympathy clearing. Loot the sword ruin before ending in danger zones.
2 Skipping crafts
The mistake: "I'll save these cards." Cards in hand at end of game = 0 VP. Cards crafted into items = 1 VP each + an item that scores you more. Always craft when you have the matching hammer.
Instead: Craft every craftable card, every turn. Mouse and Rabbit items are safest (don't power up Vagabond's competition).
3 Never going Hostile
The mistake: Aiding everyone, never attacking. You score ~5 VP/game from items + ~5 VP from Aid = 10 VP. Game ends, you lose by 20.
Instead: Mid-game, pick a Hostile target faction. Use the crossbow to flip Hostile if you weren't already. Now your battles score Infamy = 1 VP per kill = the path to 30.
4 Aiding the Alliance / Lizards
The mistake: Heavy Aid into Alliance or Lizard Cult. Each card you give them = 1 VP for them (they convert cards to VP faster than anyone). You're handing them the win.
Instead: Aid the Cats and Eyrie primarily (they get less from cards). Aid Alliance/Lizards only when their items are excellent, and stop early.
5 Misreading threat
The mistake: "The Cats are leading at 18, I'll attack them." But the Cats slow down at high VP (slots fill up). The Alliance at 13 with 8 supporters is the real threat — about to burst 15 VP.
Instead: Assess threat by faction-specific potential, not raw VP. See Matchups for the threat-read for each faction.

07Items & Damage Track

Items are everything. Each item type does something different — and gets exhausted by specific actions. Understanding the inventory mechanics deeply is what separates good Vagabonds from great ones.

The 7 item types

🥾 Boots
Used for: Move action.
Why important: Without boots, you can't leave your clearing (except via Slip). 2+ boots lets you move multiple times per turn.
⚔️ Swords
Used for: Battle action (and as combat strength).
Why important: Swords ARE your win condition late game. 3+ swords = Infamy farming. Sword count = combat threat to others.
🔨 Hammers
Used for: Crafting.
Why important: 1 hammer = 1 craft per turn. The hammer ruin item (orange-suit hammer) is the most-fought-over ruin.
🏹 Crossbow
Used for: Strike action (1 hit, no counter).
Why important: Free hit at range. Used to flip Hostile without committing to a battle. Ranger starts with one.
🍵 Tea
Used for: Adds +1 to your Birdsong refresh limit per Tea.
Why important: More items refreshed = more actions. Tea is hidden value — boring but vital for high item counts.
🎒 Bag
Used for: Holds extra items (you have an inventory limit).
Why important: Without bags, you cap at fewer items. Tinker starts with one extra; everyone else needs to craft.
🪙 Coins
Used for: Buying from Riverfolk Company services.
Why important: Conditional value. Useful only if Riverfolk are in the game. Don't seek out unless they are.

Refresh / Exhaust / Damage cycle

  • Refreshed (face-up): Item is ready to use. Spending the item flips it to exhausted.
  • Exhausted (face-down): Item is used this turn. Refreshed at start of next Birdsong (up to your refresh limit).
  • Damaged: Item taken hit in combat. Set aside on damaged track. Cannot use OR refresh until repaired.

Refresh limit (the silent constraint)

Default refresh You refresh up to 3 items per Birdsong. So 4 items but only 3 refresh = 1 stays exhausted.
Each Tea adds +1 to refresh limit. So Tea + 4 items = all refresh.

Repairing damaged items

Damaged items return to refreshed via:

  • Repair action (1 hammer + 1 matching-color card → repair 1 item). Costs an action.
  • Hideout (Ranger only): repair 3 items at once. Free action.
  • Lick wounds: end Evening alone in a forest → repair 1 damaged item. Free but takes a slow turn.
Why this matters: Damage management is your real defense. With 8 items, you can absorb 8 hits before you "die" — but each damaged item is a missed action. Plan repair into your turn cycle.

08Aid & Crafting

Aid and Crafting are your steady scoring streams in the early-mid game. They're not as bursty as combat-Infamy, but they're reliable, low-risk, and they scale with hand size.

Aid — what it actually does

Aid is a Daylight action where you give a card matching the clearing's suit to a faction in your clearing. In return:

  • The faction must give you 1 of their crafted items (they choose which)
  • You draw a card
  • You move 1 step on the friendship track with that faction

The friendship track

Path to Allied
  1. Indifferent (start) — no special status
  2. Aid 3 times → Friendly — score 1 VP per Aid going forward
  3. Aid 2 more times while Friendly → Allied — score 2 VP per Aid going forward

Becoming Allied with one faction is good for ~5–8 VP across a game. Becoming Allied with two is rare but possible if you commit hard.

Aid targets — who to befriend

Best Aid targets
  • Cats — get less out of cards than other factions, easy to keep down. Cards they receive ≈ 1 VP per card.
  • Eyrie — bird cards specifically are deceptively safe; Eyrie wants few of them anyway. Useful early to deflect the bird's aggression.
  • Riverfolk — yes you might fight them later, but early Aid gets items + cards while they're weak.
Avoid heavy Aid
  • Alliance — every card you give them ≈ 1 VP for them. Aid only if their crafted items are excellent.
  • Lizards — cards = extra actions AND points. You'd be feeding their engine.

Crafting

Crafting is the same mechanic the Cats use, but for Vagabond it's doubly good: you score the VP and get the item. To craft:

  • Have a card with crafting requirements (suit + image)
  • Have hammer items of the matching colors (orange/blue/yellow/green corresponding to suits)
  • Spend the card → place item in your inventory + score the card's VP value (1–3)
Hammer color matters: The starting hammer (Tinker / from ruin) is one specific color. To craft cards of other colors, you need additional hammers (which other factions craft and you can Aid for). This is why heavy item-crafting on the table = good for the Vagabond.

Crafting priority for Vagabond

  • Mouse and rabbit VP cards — safe, useful, won't power up rivals (Cats/Eyrie)
  • Avoid fox VP cards if your group is item-conscious — fox items are powerful general gear (Sword, Crossbow). Crafting them puts them on the table where opponents can grab via Aid
  • Wait — actually for you, fox items are great. The "avoid fox" rule is for the Cats. As Vagabond, you want every item you can grab.

09Quests

Quest cards represent specific tasks. Each Quest card has a suit (the clearing it must be done in) and 2 item requirements (you must exhaust those items to complete it).

The Quest mechanic

  • Quest cards are face-up next to the board (3 active)
  • Daylight action: be in a clearing matching the Quest's suit, exhaust the required items, complete the quest
  • First quest in a suit: draw 1 card OR score 1 VP (your choice)
  • Each subsequent quest in that suit: score 2 VP

When to quest

Take if
  • You're already in the right suit clearing (no detour cost)
  • The required items are already exhausted or you have spares
  • You have multiple quests of the same suit available (the 2 VP scaling kicks in)
Skip if
  • Requires major detour (3+ extra moves)
  • The required items are critical for combat this turn
  • You're in early game and item-collection is more important
BGG verdict: Quests are opportunistic. Don't build a strategy around them — but don't ignore them either. Take the freebies when they're in your path. Lean toward "card" on the first quest of each suit (more cards = more crafts later); take VP after.

10Relationships

Every faction has a relationship status with you, tracked on your faction board:

Indifferent Default. No bonuses, no penalties. You can still battle but won't score Infamy.
Friendly After 3 Aids to that faction. Each subsequent Aid scores 1 VP.
Allied After 5 total Aids (3 + 2). Each Aid scores 2 VP. Plus, allied warriors don't trigger Hostile when you battle nearby.
Hostile After you attack their warriors (without Aiding them recently). All future battles against them score 1 VP per warrior killed (Infamy).

How you become Hostile

  • Battling their warriors when not Friendly/Allied with them
  • Killing one of their warriors with the crossbow Strike
  • Note: Removing buildings/tokens (not warriors) does NOT trigger Hostile

The flip — when to go Hostile

Most Vagabond games begin with Indifferent toward all factions. Around turn 4–6, you should pick a target to become Hostile to. Considerations:

Pick a Hostile target with
  • Lots of warriors clustered — Lizards, Riverfolk, sometimes Eyrie
  • Lightly defended high-value pieces (gardens, sympathy, Roosts)
  • NOT the leading scorer — they'll target you back. Pick #2 or #3 in score.

Hostile penalty

The cost: Once Hostile to a faction, when you move through their warriors' clearings, you take an extra "passage cost" — meaning Hostile factions can slow your movement. Slip movement bypasses this.

Becoming Friendly with someone you were Hostile to

You can recover from Hostile by Aiding 3 times (now those Aid cards are mostly used to repair the relationship, not to score). This is rarely worth it — once Hostile, stay Hostile and farm Infamy.

11Mid-Game Pivot

Around turn 4–6, your strategy shifts. The opening was about accumulation (items). The mid-game is about scoring with what you accumulated.

TL;DR. Once you have 2+ swords, transition from defensive looting to offensive scoring. Pick a Hostile target. Begin Infamy farming. Keep aiding non-threats for steady VP. Maintain ~2 craft VP per turn.

The pivot signals

  • You have 2+ swords (now safe from most attacks)
  • All ruins are looted (no more "free" items from exploration)
  • One faction has emerged as a clear threat or scoring leader
  • You have a hand of cards ready to spend on Aid/Craft

The mid-game scoring stack

Each turn you should aim for 3–5 VP via combination:

  • 1 VP from crafting (1 item from a card you have)
  • 1–2 VP from Aiding (Friendly/Allied bonuses)
  • 2–4 VP from combat (Infamy on Hostile target)
  • 0–2 VP from Quest (if a Quest happens to be in your path)

The combat priority

Attack if
  • Target is Hostile to you (Infamy scoring active)
  • Target has 2+ warriors stacked (multiple kills per battle)
  • You have all swords refreshed
  • Killing the warriors also removes a high-value building (extra VP for some Vagabonds)
Sample turn 6 — mid-game in full swing
You're Arbiter at 14 VP with 3 swords, hostile to Lizards. The Lizards have 3 warriors + 1 garden in a rabbit clearing (lightly defended).
Birdsong: Refresh items, slip into the rabbit clearing.
Daylight: Battle (kill 2 warriors, +2 VP). Battle (kill 1 warrior + garden building, +1 VP for kill, +1 VP for building). Aid the Cats with a leftover card, scoring 1 VP at Friendly. Craft a 1-VP item.
Evening: Draw 2 cards.
Net: 6 VP this turn. Lizards down 3 warriors and a garden.

12Matchups & Threat Assessment

Each matchup answers: how to read their threat, when to Aid them, when to attack them. Score doesn't equal threat — assess by faction-specific potential.

Marquise (Cats)
Threat read: Count their open building slots × 2 ≈ remaining VP potential. Watch for dominance setup (warriors clustered for corner takes).
Aid them: Yes — cards are mid-value to them. Befriending Cats is the safest aid path.
Attack them: Late game when slots fill up — Cats have many board pieces to harvest for Infamy. Don't break Cats fully though, they keep Alliance in line.
Eyrie
Threat read: Count Roosts × position on score track. Easiest faction to assess — their VP is mostly transparent. Watch for Despot leader (sudden VP burst from removing pieces).
Aid them: Yes — bird cards specifically are safe (they want few of them). Eyrie crafts rarely so they'll feed you items.
Attack them: Midgame if they're consolidating. Each Roost destroyed denies them future VP.
Woodland Alliance
Threat read: Count cards in supporters + officers + bases. Even a low-VP Alliance with 8 supporters is about to burst 15 VP. The most-underestimated faction by score.
Aid them: Carefully. Their items are good but every card you give them ≈ 1 VP. Don't max friendship.
Attack them: Sympathy tokens are 1 VP each (low risk targets). Crossbow lets you bypass Guerrilla War. But beware Outrage — every battle gives them a card.
Lizard Cult
Threat read: Count gardens + acolytes. Gardens score every turn AND fuel sanctify. A 6-garden Lizard is winning even at low VP.
Aid them: Almost never. Cards = extra Lizard actions AND points. You're feeding the worst engine for you.
Attack them: YES. Lightly-defended gardens are juicy (high VP per battle). Their warriors don't fight back well. Prime Infamy farm.
Riverfolk (Otters)
Threat read: They can suddenly drop 5+ warriors anywhere via recruit. Volatile threat — judge by their hand size and warrior supply.
Aid them: Tempting (they craft well). Don't over-feed cards.
Attack them: YES. Killing Otter warriors denies them VP and gets you Infamy. Their lack of defended territory makes them safe targets. Best Hostile candidate.
Corvid Conspiracy
Threat read: Look at face-down plot count × position. Bombs and snares can wipe warriors but you have no warriors to lose.
Aid them: Neutral. They craft moderately.
Attack them: Decent option. Plotter warriors when exposed = Infamy. Don't reveal plots accidentally.
Underground Duchy
Threat read: Crown VP track + minister sway. Hard to read — they swing.
Aid them: Cautious yes — they craft.
Attack them: Tunnel-defended clearings are tough. Pick weak frontier targets.
Keepers in Iron
Threat read: Relics recovered + waystation chains.
Aid them: Neutral.
Attack them: Decent — waystations destroyed = denied VP.
The threat-assessment heuristic: Look at what each faction needs to score. Cats need open slots. Eyrie needs Roosts. Alliance needs supporters + bases. Lizards need gardens. Riverfolk need card spending. Multiply those resources by remaining VP availability — that's the real threat, not the current score.

13Coalition Path

Coalition is a special play where you and another player declare a partnership: if either of you reaches 30 VP first, you both win.

When Vagabond should coalition

  • You're far behind on score with little chance to win solo (unusual but possible)
  • One other player is close to winning via dominance — you can support their final push
  • Two other players are fighting each other, leaving a third on track to win — coalition with the third

The Cat-Vagabond coalition (most common)

Cats often coalition with the Vagabond before activating a dominance card. Why:

  • Vagabond has flexible mobility (Slip)
  • Vagabond can quickly attack a key clearing (e.g. an Alliance base) to remove threats to the Cat dominance
  • Cats neutralize Vagabond's main threat (the Cat's huge army)

Scoundrel + Cats specifically

The Scorched Earth play BGG-mentioned scenario: Cats need to dominate but one path is blocked. Scoundrel uses Scorched Earth to wipe a key clearing, breaking the path. Cats activate dominance unopposed. Both win.
One of the strongest closing plays in Root.
The trade: Coalition = giving up your independent win. But Vagabond's path to 30 is slow; coalitioning to a faction at 22 VP that can sprint to 30 is often higher EV than soloing from 18.

14Mental Model

Stop thinking
"What's my next action?"
Start thinking
"Who is closest to winning, and how can I delay them while scoring myself?"

You're not playing a faction strategy — you're playing a table-state strategy. Your job is to assess relative power every turn and intervene where you score most.

Heuristic "If you've been ignored for 3 turns, you're winning."

15Advanced Tactics

Sympathy as safe haven
Alliance sympathy clearings deter most opponents (they trigger Outrage if attacked there). End your turn here when vulnerable.
Slip to forest = perfect cover
Slip is free movement, doesn't trigger Hostile, and forests have no warriors. End in a forest to avoid all aggression.
Exploit overburdened factions
Eyrie just turmoiled? Lizards just lost half their gardens? Alliance just bursted? They're depleted — easy Infamy farm.
Crossbow flip-trick
Need to become Hostile but don't want to commit a full battle? Use the crossbow Strike to kill 1 warrior. Now Hostile, no counterattack.
Tinker → Favor crafting
Tinker can search the discard pile. Combine with hammer access = only Vagabond capable of crafting Favor cards. Game-winning move if pulled off.
Damage rotation
Take damage on items you don't need this turn. Boots damaged but you weren't moving anyway? Free damage absorbed.
Aid for items, not friendship
Sometimes you Aid just for a single item, not pursuing Friendly status. Item + card draw + 1 friendship step is fine value even at 0 friendship VP.
Don't telegraph swords
Players can see your sword count. Hold off on collecting the 4th sword visibly — opponents start noticing the threat at 3 swords.

17Glossary

Every term used in this aid, defined. Quick reference for jargon.

Vagabond Pieces & Items

Pawn
Your single Vagabond meeple. The only piece you have on the board. Moves between clearings. When attacked, hits go to your items, not the pawn itself.
Item
Token in your inventory. Comes in 7 types: torch, boots, sword, hammer, crossbow, tea, bag, coin. Each has a face-up "refreshed" or face-down "exhausted" state. Damaged items go on a separate damaged track.
Refreshed
Item state where it can be used. Default starting state. Set face-up.
Exhausted
Item state after being used in an action this turn. Set face-down. Refreshed at start of next Birdsong, up to your refresh limit.
Damaged
Item state after being hit in combat. Set on the damaged track. Cannot be used or refreshed until repaired.
Refresh limit
Maximum items refreshed per Birdsong. Default 3. +1 per Tea item you have.

Vagabond Actions

Move
Daylight action. Exhaust 1 boot. Move to an adjacent clearing or forest. If moving into a Hostile faction's clearing or with their pieces present, costs an extra boot.
Slip
Birdsong free action (1 per turn). Move to an adjacent clearing or forest WITHOUT exhausting a boot, AND without triggering Hostile passage costs. Vagabond's signature mobility tool.
Battle
Daylight action. Exhaust 1 sword. Battle in a clearing where you have your pawn. You're the attacker; deal hits = sword count.
Strike
Daylight action. Exhaust 1 crossbow. Deal 1 free hit to a clearing within range. No counter-attack. Doesn't directly score Infamy but kills count for Infamy if Hostile.
Aid
Daylight action. Discard a card matching the clearing's suit. Take 1 of that faction's crafted items. Draw a card. Move +1 on friendship track with that faction.
Quest
Daylight action. Be in a clearing matching a Quest card's suit. Exhaust the items the Quest requires. Score VP or draw a card.
Craft
Daylight free action (no item cost). Spend a card matching your hammer color to gain that card's item AND score the card's VP. Best scoring rate per card in the game for Vagabond.
Repair
Daylight action. Exhaust a hammer + spend a card matching the damaged item's color. Move 1 damaged item back to refreshed.
Lick wounds
Evening action (free). If alone in a forest, repair 1 damaged item. Slow recovery for when you can't afford a Repair action.
Special action
Each Vagabond has a unique once-per-turn or once-per-game ability: Steal (Thief), Tinker (Tinker), Hideout (Ranger), Instigate (Vagrant), Protector (Arbiter), Scorched Earth (Scoundrel).

Relationships & Scoring

Indifferent
Default relationship status with all factions. No bonuses, no penalties. Battling them once switches you to Hostile.
Friendly
Achieved after 3 Aids to a faction. Each subsequent Aid scores 1 VP. Cannot become Hostile to Friendly factions accidentally.
Allied
Achieved after 5 total Aids (3 + 2). Each Aid scores 2 VP. Allied factions are protected partners — battling near them doesn't trigger Hostile.
Hostile
Achieved by killing a faction's warrior while not Friendly/Allied. Combat against them now scores 1 VP per warrior killed (Infamy). They get 1 VP each time they remove your items in combat.
Infamy
VP scored by killing warriors of a Hostile faction. Vagabond's primary mid-late game scoring path. 1 VP per warrior (most Vagabonds), or 2 VP for the Vagrant in specific Instigate cases.
Friendship track
Per-faction track on your faction board showing Indifferent → Friendly → Allied progression. Aiding moves you forward; attacking can move you to Hostile.

Game-Wide Concepts

Ruin
Pre-placed game-start tokens (4 total) on the map. Each contains an item. Vagabond can use Explore action (free) to extract the item. Once explored, the ruin is removed. Other factions can't loot ruins.
Hammer color
Hammers come in 4 colors (orange/blue/green/yellow) corresponding to crafting card suits (fox/rabbit/mouse/bird). Crafting requires a hammer of the matching color.
Forest
Spaces between clearings, no faction can place pieces there normally. Vagabond CAN move to forests. Useful for safety and licking wounds.
Coalition
Optional partnership where two players share a victory. If either reaches 30 VP, both win. Often the Vagabond's late-game pivot if solo path fails.
Birdsong / Daylight / Evening
Three phases of every player's turn. Birdsong = refresh, Slip. Daylight = main actions. Evening = lick wounds, draw cards.

Other Faction Terms (Referenced)

Sympathy
Alliance token in a clearing. Triggers Outrage when removed. Useful safe-haven for Vagabond — opponents avoid attacking sympathy clearings.
Outrage
Alliance ability — when their sympathy is removed, they get a card. Why opponents avoid sympathy clearings.
Dominance card
Special victory cards (3 corners / one suit / etc). Cats often coalition with Vagabond before activating one to neutralize Vagabond's threat.
Decree
Eyrie's mandatory action stack. Predictable — lets you anticipate Eyrie movements and avoid being in their forced-action clearings.
Outcast suit
Lizards' designated bonus suit. Watch for it; Lizards may be aggressively expanding into outcast clearings, making them dense Infamy targets.
