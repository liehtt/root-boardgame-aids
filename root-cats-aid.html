03PRIORITY 1Opening (Turns 1–3)

Goal: stable, connected, defensible engine. Messed-up openings lose more Cat games than anything else.

Turn 1 — Ignition

Default
Build → Overwork → Build
  1. Sawmill (safe clearing)
  2. Overwork (if matching card)
  3. Recruiter (chokepoint)
No Overwork card
Build → Build → (March?)
  • 2 builds > anything else
  • March only: secure 3-slot / wood / block Alliance
Placement Sawmill = safe clearing · Recruiter = chokepoint · prefer 2–3 slot clearings.

Turn 2 — Stabilize vs Scale

Default
Recruiter → Recruit → Build
Get one recruiter per turn. Goal: 3 recruiters down → baseline 3 cards/turn.
Rabbit Workshop
Build a workshop only if you can craft Cobbler / Command Warren / Better Burrow Bank this turn.
Alliance pressure
If the Alliance is "in your business" early, propose a quiet truce — neither of you wants a Red Queen's race this early.

Turn 3 — Engine Online

Default Recruit → Build → Build
Disrupted Battle → Build → Recruit
Bird card Extra action = 2–3 builds. Treat birds as gifts.
Turn order If you go after Alliance, the opening sympathy may force you to settle for just a sawmill turn 1. After Eyrie/Vagabond is fine — they don't disrupt your build.

07PRIORITY 2Combat — Pick Your Battles

🚫 Don't fight without a payoff. ✅ But don't be passive.

In 1v1s the Cats win almost every fight — but you only have 3 actions. Each wasted battle = 1 lost build.

Fight when…

  1. Removes Alliance sympathy (every time, before Martial Law lapses)
  2. Eyrie roost in their recruit suit — kill last roost of a suit → forced turmoil
  3. Lizard garden, especially lightly defended
  4. Enables a build or restores Rule
  5. Riverfolk stack of 2+ in one clearing
Never
  • Attack an Alliance base head-on — you'll lose the action war. Move warriors in to blockade instead, never fight
  • Pure "slow them down" with no gain
  • Total war on Eyrie — keep them down but not out (they help police minor factions)
ESSENTIAL

06PRIORITY 3Movement — March with Purpose

March moves 2 — make every step count.

Cats spend the first half of the game in free fall. Every move must protect a goal, not just expand.

March when…

  1. Smother an Alliance base — move warriors in, don't fight, blockade
  2. Reinforce a recruiter clearing you'll dominate
  3. Restore Rule for next turn's wood
  4. Cobbler-free move to rearrange after a strike (T3+ if crafted)
SW/NW corner The 5-path fox clearing is your spine — move through it, never let it fall.

02Action Economy

Good Action
  • Generates VP now (build)
  • Enables future builds
  • Prevents engine collapse
Bad Action
  • Fighting without gain
  • Moving without purpose
  • Crafting without impact
Truth Real resource = ACTIONS, not wood.

05Crafting — Rabbit-First

Why Crafting is not an action. Rabbit crafts give back the actions you don't have.

S-Tier — Rabbit (build 2 rabbit workshops)

  • Command Warren — start a battle at start of Daylight (free sympathy removal, free counterattack)
  • Cobbler — free move every turn (rearrange, pull back, maintain Martial Law)
  • Better Burrow Bank — extra cards = more actions, troops, crafts

Strong Pickups — Other Suits

  • Codebreakers (mouse, 1 cost) — see opponents' hands, scout Favors/Ambush
  • Tax Collector (mouse) — 1 warrior → 1 card, great trade for Cats
  • Scouting Party (mouse) — safely clear sympathy/buildings with one warrior
  • Armorers (fox) — only when gearing up for the dominance fight; negates a bad roll
  • Royal Claim (bird) — late-game burst with the 4-workshop setup

Workshop Build Order

Default 2 rabbit, then 1 mouse (or 1 mouse, then 2 rabbit)
4th workshop 1 mouse · 2 rabbit · 1 fox = nearly every craft you'll want, including Royal Claim
Avoid 1 lone rabbit workshop (not enough leverage). Skip fox crafts early — most are bird cards or feed the Vagabond.
CORE

11Defense — Keep & Field Hospitals

Protect Keep at all costs
Lose Keep → no Field Hospitals → game over. Watch for sneaky removals (Favor of the Mice, dominance attacks).

Field Hospitals — soak losses

  • With 5 recruiters down + Field Hospitals you can soak 8–10 losses in a round and retake a clearing
  • Discard cards liberally — they're cheap and you draw lots
  • vs Lizards: discard to manipulate the outcast suit in your favor
Tempo If a dominance push fails turn 1, you can usually retake. Don't panic.

10Late Game — Closing the Win

Dominance Path (default — 2/3 of Cat wins)

  • Place recruiters on the clearings you'll dominate — replenish if turn-1 push fails
  • Soften opponents before activating — fight without scoring focus, take inevitable battles on your terms
  • Don't panic if blocked turn 1 — opponents who throw everything at one clearing leave themselves thin. With 5 recruiters + Field Hospitals you soak 8–10 losses and retake
  • Mouse / Fox / Bird suit are most common targets depending on starting corner

Coalition with Vagabond

If trailing on score before activating dominance: coalition with Vagabond to neutralize their threat. Pick whoever has the lowest score.

Score Path (fallback only)

  • Craft well — don't let wood pile up (Eyrie/Corvids will burn it)
  • 2–3 builds in a single turn powered by bird cards / pre-cleared slots
  • Tiny stockpile for final-turn burst is OK; large stockpile = target

15Matchups (All 8 Factions)

Eyrie
Threat: Decree grinds your warriors; biggest 1v1 rival.
Counter: Watch the Recruit column — if they have only 1–2 roosts of a suit, kill those roosts to force turmoil. Keep front line solid; back line is hard for them to reach.
Don't: Wage total war — you both need each other to police minor factions.
Woodland Alliance
Threat: Sympathy + revolts; bog you down with their officers.
Counter: Smother — never attack a base head-on (you only kill 1+ warrior ~25% of rolls). Move warriors in to blockade bases so they can't move out. Trigger Martial Law every time sympathy lands.
Goal: Make your territory so unappealing they slow down someone else.
Vagabond
Threat: Snowballs once geared with swords.
Counter: Beat them up early before swords pile up. If crafting victory, only craft rabbit & mouse (don't feed their kit) — they're a fine card-engine ally. Hostile = your territory is molasses for them.
Coalition: Before activating dominance, offer coalition if you're last in points.
Lizard Cult
Threat: Powerhouse since rebalance; ignores defenses, attacks soft targets.
Counter: Keep them stuck on one suit and deny that suit's dominance card. Check the outcast suit before playing any dominance — and remember you can look through the Lost Souls pile any time. Discard cards via Field Hospitals to push the outcast suit your way.
Ally up: Team with Eyrie/Alliance against them.
Riverfolk
Threat: Tons of actions — they can drop a dozen warriors in one turn and erase your back line.
Counter: Control the table conversation — between all opponents, no more than 2 services bought per turn. Kill any clearing with 2+ Riverfolk warriors — putting warriors on the board is their only expensive thing.
Corvid Conspiracy
Threat: plots disrupt · bombs wipe stacks.
Counter: expose plots near buildings · no stacks on plots · accept exposure cost.
Fight: plot adjacent to 2+ buildings.
Underground Duchy
Threat: burrows · crown scoring · ministers snowball.
Counter: race crown · deny ministerial suit · keep ministers swayable.
Fight: tunnels in your core.
Keepers in Iron
Threat: relic recovery · waystation VP chains.
Counter: block relic corridors · recover relics yourself · deny waystation chain.
Fight: waystation in a 3-slot you need.

16Starting Corner (Autumn)

Criteria: rabbit crafting access · accessibility (March efficiency) · defensibility of Keep.

SW — Best
Adjacent 5-path fox clearing = your spine. Two rabbit clearings adjacent for crafting. Reach 3 mouse / fox / bird clearings for any dominance.
Hold the 5-path fox clearing at all costs — lose it, you're split.
NW — Flexible
Like SW: focus the 5-path fox clearing. Strong rabbit access. Any dominance possible. Vulnerable at higher player counts.
NE — Default but trap
Decent rabbit access + concentrated recruiting on home + 3-slot mouse to the south. Vulnerable to Favor of the Mice; easy to be isolated.
SE — Worst for crafting
Only 1 rabbit slot, no rabbit within 2 moves — Favor of the Rabbits is easy for opponents. Skip rabbit; fortify 3 nearby mouse clearings and aim for mouse or fox dominance.

18Advanced Tactics

3-warrior threshold
Garrison clearings with exactly 3 warriors — enacts Martial Law and absorbs one battle action without breaking. Cheaper than a stack of 5.
Sawmill adjacent to Keep
Early sawmills next to Keep maximize wood-chain safety. Field Hospitals reinforces them instantly.
Recruiter as Alliance trap
Move a recruiter into a sympathy-cleared clearing — opponents must spend actions to dislodge it, and you regenerate.
25-warrior cap
Once at population cap, stop recruiting — convert those actions into builds or battles instead.
Field Hospitals = card cycle
Discard low-value cards to revive warriors. It's a cheap card-cycle on top of resilience.
Riverfolk mercenary trap
Warriors spent on blue cards can be hired by Riverfolk and dropped against you. Don't fund the gun pointed at your back line.
Don't telegraph alliances
Subtly: don't craft Vagabond's preferred items even if "free VP." Visible favoritism stacks the table on you.
Force Eyrie collapse early
Don't passively wait for turmoil — actively prune roosts when their decree is brittle and their lead is still small.
